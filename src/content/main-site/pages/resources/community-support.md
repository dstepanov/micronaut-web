# Community Support

## Getting Started

### [Micronaut Launch](https://micronaut.io/launch/)

**This is the easiest way to create a new project with the latest version of the Micronaut framework.** Just select the type of application you would like to create along with the language, build system, test framework, and features you would like to include. You can download a new project with the provided configuration by selecting “Generate Project.”

### [Download](https://micronaut.io/download/)

Create new Micronaut projects by downloading and installing the Framework.

### [User Guide](https://docs.micronaut.io/latest/guide/index.html)

Review the definitive guide to developing applications with the Framework.

### [Documentation](https://micronaut.io/docs/)

Access documentation for the various modules that make up the Framework.

### [Guides](https://micronaut.io/guides/)

Maximize your productivity with these step-by-step tutorials written by the Framework’s core developers.

### [On-Demand Webinars](https://micronaut.io/category/webinar/)

View recordings of past technical webinars to gain a deeper understanding of the Framework and expand your skills with live coding examples.

### [Microcasts](https://micronaut.io/category/microcast/)

Watch short tutorials designed to help you maximize your productivity with the Framework and confidently contribute to the ecosystem.

## Contributing to the Project

### [Contributor License Agreement (CLA)](https://cla-assistant.io/micronaut-projects/micronaut-core)

Everyone who contributes to the project is asked to review and sign our CLA before getting started.

Interested in contributing your skills to enhancing the Framework? Join our [Micronaut Discord Community](https://discord.com/invite/9xRFsHv98T) and let us know. The Micronaut development team can provide guidance on how best to tackle a particular problem, collaborate on implementation ideas, and discuss the semantics or scope of a proposed change.

### [Source](http://github.com/micronaut-projects/micronaut-core)

Visit the Micronaut project on GitHub.

### [Issue Tracker](https://github.com/micronaut-projects/micronaut-core/issues)

The Micronaut project uses GitHub Issues to report and track bugs, feature enhancements, and new features.

If you’re uncertain about whether or not a particular behavior represents a bug or if a particular feature exists and would like to discuss it before submitting the request, our [Discord channel](https://discord.com/invite/9xRFsHv98T) is a good forum to discuss the topic.

### [Feature Requests](https://github.com/micronaut-projects/micronaut-core/issues/new)

The best way to make a feature request is to complete this form.

### [Discussions](https://github.com/micronaut-projects/micronaut-core/discussions)

If you’ve come across something you think might be a bug, but you’re not sure if you should submit a formal issue, feel free to bring it up in our discussion board.

### [Documentation Updates](https://docs.micronaut.io/snapshot/guide/index.html)

We want our documentation to be clear and easy to follow for users worldwide. Please don’t hesitate to help us make improvements, fix typos or broken language, clarify complicated sections, add new material, and anything else you feel will be helpful to other Micronaut users.

To edit a section of the documentation, sign into your GitHub account and access the AsciiDoc file with the applicable “Improve this doc” button.

### [Community Guidelines](https://micronaut.io/community-guidelines/)

We respectfully ask that community members adhere to a simple set of guidelines to ensure that everyone participating in the community and the project, whether as a contributor, a user, or an interested bystander, feels welcome and supported.

## Troubleshooting & Support

### [Support Schedule](https://micronaut.io/support-schedule/)

The Micronaut Foundation is committed to the continued innovation, development, maintenance, and support of the Micronaut framework.

Our goal is to provide users of the Micronaut framework the support they need, while maintaining as much focus as possible on innovation. The level and type of support available for earlier versions will change, as the Framework continues to evolve. For more information about the level of support available for each version of the Micronaut framework, please see our [support schedule](https://micronaut.io/support-schedule/).

### [Consulting & Commercial Support](https://micronaut.io/support/)

We understand that different organizations have different support needs. With our support options below, we can help your team make the most of the features available in the Micronaut framework and add another layer of protection to the investment in your critical business applications.

Unsure of which package is right for your group? Please [contact us](mailto:info@micronautfoundation.io) to review your current needs and we can help find the right mix of support options for your organization!

### [Stack Overflow](https://stackoverflow.com/questions/tagged/micronaut)

You can ask technical questions and get guidance regarding specific programming problems on Stack Overflow.

The Micronaut development team makes a priority of monitoring this channel. If you have a specific technical question, post your question to Stack Overflow using the #micronaut or #micronaut-data tag.

Questions posed on Stack Overflow generally have specific answers; if you’re looking to discuss a broader topic,[join us on Discord](https://discord.com/invite/9xRFsHv98T).

### [Professional Training](https://micronaut.io/professional-training/)

The Micronaut Foundation is pleased to offer professional development training to support our clients and community. These live, instructor-led training workshops, are designed to help your team quickly master the Micronaut framework. All workshops are developed and taught by members of the core Micronaut development team.

Private training engagements are also available. Private engagements can be adapted, expanded, combined, and tailored to cover the specific skills your team requires to achieve your goals. These offerings can be conducted online, at your location, or in Object Computing’s training lab located in St. Louis, Missouri.

Please let us know if you would like to discuss a private offering for your team by filling out the form below.

Open-enrollment courses are offered periodically throughout the year; these are a great, cost-effective option for organizations that have an immediate need to train a small number of employees, as well as for individuals looking to boost their skills.
